THE MALASANA POSE

Malasana is also called Garland Pose. It is, to put it simply, a squat. Squatting comes naturally to kids and people who work in the fields. However living a sedentary lifestyle makes it painful and uncomfortable. This should not discourage you because it is, in fact, most beneficial to those with sedentary lifestyle.
You must make sure to keep your stomach and bowels empty before you practice this asana. This asana activates the digestive system, tones the belly, improves the posture, increases flexibility in your ankles and knees.
LEVEL:Basic STRETCHES: Thighs, Hamstrings, Groin, Lower back
STRENGTHENS: Lower back, Hips, Digestive system

FASCIAL CONDITIONING

Fascia is a broad term for the connective tissue that weaves throughout the entire body to stabilize and bind the muscles, bones, and organs together. It involves using dynamic, rebounding movements to condition and reorganize our fascia into supple, reactive bands, dramatically increasing range of motion, athleticism, and mobility.
When fascia loses its elasticity due to inactivity, lack of mobility, or aging, our bodies lose their youthful bounce and we feel achy, sore and unable to perform optimally in yoga or other physical activities. As we move dynamically in these poses, we allow the rebound to return us to our neutral position. By training the fascia this way, we not only feel healthier and more mobile, but over time we increase our balance, proprioception, and reflex.

REVOLVED HEAD TO KNEE POSE

Revolved Head to Knee Pose is an intense seated twist pose that offers more benefits than a standing pose. It was designed to relieve back pain and should be performed in the second half of your yoga session after your body has had time to warm up.
-It stretches the hamstrings, shoulders and spine
-It relieves lower back pain
-It improves breathing
-It reduces anxiety, stress and insomnia
